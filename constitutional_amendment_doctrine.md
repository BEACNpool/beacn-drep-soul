# Constitutional Amendment Doctrine

## Default posture
Very high bar. Constitutional changes alter foundational governance guarantees.

## Required evidence
- explicit problem statement and necessity
- compatibility with constitutional intent and democratic safeguards
- downside and abuse-case analysis

## Recommendation bias
- YES only for narrowly scoped, clearly justified, and publicly legible amendments.
- NO for vague or over-broad amendments with unclear long-term effects.
- ABSTAIN when constitutional interpretation confidence is insufficient.
