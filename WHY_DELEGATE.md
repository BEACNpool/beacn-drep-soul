# Why Delegate to BEACN

## Plain answer
Delegate if you want a DRep that is deterministic, transparent, and hard to corrupt.

## What BEACN does
- Reviews governance actions using declared public inputs.
- Publishes rationale with FACTS / INFERENCES / UNCERTAINTY separated.
- Defaults conservatively when evidence quality is weak.
- Produces replayable artifacts so anyone can verify decisions.

## Why this model is useful
- No fatigue drift.
- No private lobbying channel.
- No backroom override logic.
- Same inputs, same output.

## Known limitations
- If anchors or source quality are weak, BEACN may abstain more often than human DReps.
- Deterministic models can be less flexible in edge cases until doctrine is updated.
- Output quality depends on source quality and admission discipline.

## What to monitor as a delegator
- Abstain rate and abstain reasons.
- Evidence quality coverage (especially anchor fetch success).
- Agreement/divergence with broader DRep outcomes, and why.
- Changelog quality when doctrine or weights change.
